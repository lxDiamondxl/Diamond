Calories in Different Fruits

Fruits are often described as a healthy, nutritious, sweet, & delicious snack, but did you know that they can also have a huge impact on our calorie intake? While many fruits are low in calories, some can be surprisingly high in calories. A Single cup of strawberries contains only 50 calories, but a single banana can pack up to 105 calories. The same as how a medium-sized apple contains around 95 calories, while a serving of 1/4 cup of dried apricots can have up to 170 calories. Eating an excessive amounts of fruit cause an overload of calories, this can lead to gaining weight & other health problems. However, this doesn't mean we should completely stop eating fruit. Instead, it's important to know the portion sizes & balance our fruit intake with other nutrient-dense foods. By doing this, we can have the benefits of fruits' vitamins, minerals, & antioxidants while keeping our calorie intake in check. This is called moderation!

Small Fruits

  • Blueberries (1 cup): 85 calories
  • Cranberries (1 cup): 50 calories
  • Raspberries (1 cup): 64 calories
  • Strawberries (1 cup): 50 calories
  • Blackberries (1 cup): 64 calories
  • Grapes (1 cup): 60 calories

Medium Fruits

  • Apple (1 medium): 95 calories
  • Banana (1 medium): 105 calories
  • Pear (1 medium): 62 calories
  • Peach (1 medium): 68 calories
  • Plum (1 medium): 63 calories
  • Apricot (1 medium): 51 calories

Large Fruits

  • Avocado (1 large): 320 calories
  • Orange (1 large): 136 calories
  • Pineapple (1 large): 165 calories
  • Watermelon (1 large): 110 calories
  • Grapefruit (1 large): 74 calories

Tropical Fruits

  • Papaya (1 medium): 55 calories
  • Kiwi (1 medium): 42 calories
  • Guava (1 medium): 51 calories
  • Passionfruit (1 medium): 52 calories
  • Mango (1 small): 100 calories
